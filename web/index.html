Get a divorce: step-by-step

How to file for divorce if you’re in England or Wales.

There’s a different process if you’re responding to a divorce petition or if you’re in Scotland or Northern Ireland.

  1. Step 1 Get support and advice

    You can get help dealing with your divorce and counselling to help you through the process.

    1. Get support and advice from Relate
    2. Find a counsellor

  2. Step 2 Check if you can get divorced

    Your marriage must meet certain legal requirements for you to be able to get a divorce.

    1. Check if you can get a divorce
    2. Check the grounds for divorce
    3. Get legal advice if you want help with the divorce process

  3. Step 3 Make arrangements for children, money and property

    1. Make arrangements for your children
    2. Divide your money and property

    You have to tell some government organisations that you're getting divorced if:

    • you get benefits
    • your visa is based on your marriage
    • you get State Pension

    You can also check if your divorce will affect:

    • benefits you're eligible for
    • whether you can live in your current home

  4. Step 4 File for divorce

    1. File your divorce petition online or by post £550
    2. Get help with court fees
    3. Get legal advice
    4. Get help if your husband or wife can't make decisions for themselves

  5. Step 5 Send the court more details

    You need to apply for a 'decree nisi' and give the court more information about why your marriage has broken down.

    1. Apply for a decree nisi

  6. Step 6 Finalise your divorce

    You need to apply for a 'decree absolute' to finalise your divorce. You can do this 6 weeks and 1 day after the date of your decree nisi.

    1. Apply for a decree absolute

    Once it's approved, the court will send you both a copy of the decree absolute and your divorce will be complete.
